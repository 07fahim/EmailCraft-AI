B2B COLD EMAIL BEST PRACTICES (FOLLOW STRICTLY)
===========================================

1. GREETING (CRITICAL):
   - If recipient_name is a real name → "Hi {recipient_name},"
   - If recipient_name is "NOT_PROVIDED" or empty → "Dear Hiring Manager,"
   - NEVER use brackets like [Name] or placeholders

2. SUBJECT LINE (40-55 characters):
   - Professional AND curiosity-inducing
   - MUST reference their company name OR specific role
   - Create intrigue - make them WANT to open
   
   HIGH-CONVERTING SUBJECT EXAMPLES:
     * "Alternative to hiring your {role}" (curiosity + relevance)
     * "{company_name}'s {role} - a faster option" (specific + intriguing)
     * "Question about {company_name}'s {role} search" (curiosity)
     * "Thought for {company_name}'s engineering team" (personalized)
     * "Skip the {role} hiring process?" (curiosity + pain point)
   
   OKAY BUT BORING:
     * "Regarding {company_name}'s {role} opening" (too formal)
     * "Supporting {company_name}'s {role} needs" (not intriguing)
   
   AVOID: 
     * "Quick question..." (overused, feels spammy)
     * ALL CAPS, "FREE", exclamation marks
     * Clickbait or overly clever subjects
     * Generic subjects without company/role reference

3. OPENING (2 sentences max):
   - Reference THEM, not you
   - Show you did research (mention their job posting, role, company)
   - DON'T start with "My name is..." or "I am..." or "We are..."
   - DO start with their situation: "I noticed...", "Saw that...", "Your team is..."

4. VALUE PROPOSITION (2-3 sentences):
   - Focus on THEIR pain point: hiring takes 3-6 months, uncertain results
   - Position your solution: immediate expertise without hiring risk
   - Be specific to their role/tech stack
   - Use "you/your" more than "we/us"

5. PROOF - PORTFOLIO LINKS (CRITICAL):
   - You MUST include ALL portfolio links provided above
   - List each link on its own line for readability
   - Use a professional intro, then list ALL links
   
   CORRECT FORMAT:
     "Here's some of our relevant work:
     
     • https://github.com/user/project1
     • https://github.com/user/project2
     • https://github.com/user/project3"
   
   ALTERNATIVE FORMAT:
     "Check out our recent projects:
     https://example.com/ml-project
     https://example.com/devops-project
     https://example.com/python-project"
   
   WRONG (don't describe projects):
     "We built a Machine Learning project (https://...)"
   
   REMEMBER: Include EVERY link from the PORTFOLIO section above!

6. CTA - Call to Action (1 sentence):
   - Ask for something SMALL with SPECIFIC time commitment
   - ALWAYS include a time: "Quick 10-minute call?" or "15-minute chat this week?"
   - AVOID vague CTAs like "Worth a conversation?" - too generic!
   - Make it easy to say yes with low commitment
   - Don't be pushy
   
   GOOD CTAs:
     * "Would a quick 15-minute call work this week?"
     * "Worth a 10-minute chat to explore this?"
     * "Open to a brief 15-minute call to discuss?"
   
   BAD CTAs:
     * "Worth a conversation?" (no time commitment)
     * "Let me know if interested" (no specific ask)
     * "Can we schedule a call?" (too open-ended)

7. SIGN-OFF (CRITICAL FORMAT):
   - Format MUST be: "Best regards," then NEW LINE then {sender_name}
   - Example: "Best regards,\n\nAlex"
   - Alternative closings: "Kind regards," or "Thanks,"
   - NEVER put closing and name on same line like "Best, Alex"

===========================================
EMAIL LENGTH
===========================================

- Total: 100-150 words (shorter is better)
- 2-5 sentences of actual content
- Data shows 50-125 words get best response rates
- If you can say it in fewer words, do it

===========================================
TONE GUIDELINES
===========================================

TONE: {tone}

**professional**: Clear, respectful, business-appropriate. Not stiff.
**casual**: Relaxed, conversational. Contractions OK (you're, we've, don't).
**friendly**: Warm, personable, enthusiastic but not over-the-top.
**formal**: Traditional business style. No contractions. Polished.

===========================================
WHAT TO AVOID (WILL HURT RESPONSE RATE)
===========================================

❌ Starting with "I" or "We" or "My name is"
❌ Talking about yourself more than them
❌ Long paragraphs (max 2-3 sentences each)
❌ Corporate jargon: "leverage", "synergy", "solutions", "optimize"
❌ Spam words: FREE, URGENT, LIMITED TIME, GUARANTEED, ACT NOW
❌ Brackets or placeholders: [Name], [Company], etc.
❌ Multiple CTAs or asks
❌ Being too formal or robotic
❌ Overselling or making big claims
❌ Describing portfolio projects in email body (just include the link!)
❌ Em-dashes (—) or double hyphens (--) - use periods or commas instead
❌ Run-on sentences with dashes in the middle

===========================================
WRITING STYLE (HUMAN & NATURAL)
===========================================

✅ Write like you're emailing a colleague, not a stranger
✅ Use simple, short sentences (no complex punctuation)
✅ Break long ideas into separate sentences with periods
✅ Sound conversational, not scripted
✅ Avoid fancy punctuation marks (em-dashes, semicolons, ellipsis)

WRONG: "Hiring takes 4-6 months—and there's always risk."
RIGHT: "Hiring typically takes 4-6 months. And there's always the risk they don't work out."

WRONG: "Our team can help; we have experience in ML."
RIGHT: "Our team can help. We have deep experience in ML."
